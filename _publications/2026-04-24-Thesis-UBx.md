excerpt: 'Peer sampling services are foundational primitives in large-scale distributed systems, providing each node with a continuously refreshed, near-uniform view of the system membership. 
Gossip-based implementations are particularly well-suited to this task owing to their scalability, self-healing properties, and robustness to churn. 
However, in open, adversarial environments such as public blockchain networks, Byzantine nodes can systematically bias view exchanges, corrupting the sampling distribution and enabling attacks such as eclipse attacks, denial-of-service attacks, and manipulation of consensus protocols.
This thesis addresses the problem of Byzantine-resilient gossip-based peer sampling and focuses on proposing scalable, memory-efficient solutions.'
